The Framework.

Designing buildings around the people who use them.

A practical framework for making human experience—not compliance alone—the measure of successful acoustic design.

Our belief

People experience buildings—not acoustic specifications. Standards are essential, but they are a means to an end rather than the end itself.

Six principles

A better measure of success.

01

Start with people

Begin by understanding who will use the building—not simply its function, but its occupants and their needs.

02

Design for experience

Create spaces that feel calm, comfortable and easy to communicate in, not only spaces that achieve a numerical target.

03

Use standards intelligently

Use standards to provide confidence and consistency. Let them inform design without allowing them to replace judgement.

04

Design for inclusion

Support different ages, hearing abilities and cognitive needs. Inclusive acoustic design creates better environments for everyone.

05

Balance competing needs

Balance communication, privacy, concentration, comfort, aesthetics, operation and cost rather than maximising a single parameter.

06

Measure human outcomes

Judge success by how well the acoustic environment enables people to use, understand and enjoy the building.
